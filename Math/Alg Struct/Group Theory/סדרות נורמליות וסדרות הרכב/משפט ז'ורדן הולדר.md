Def (6.8): Two normal series of $G$
$$\left\{ e \right\} = H_0 \trianglelefteq H_1 \trianglelefteq \dots \trianglelefteq H_r = G$$
$$\left\{ e \right\} = H'_0 \trianglelefteq H'_1 \trianglelefteq \dots \trianglelefteq H'_s = G$$
are called "equivalent" when they have the same length ($s=r$) and there is a permutation $\sigma \in S_r$ s.t. $\forall i \in [r]$:
$$H_i / H_{i - 1} \cong H'_{\sigma(i)} / H'_{\sigma(i) - 1}$$
(Meaning, if we define $K_i := H_i / H_{i - 1}$ and $K'_i := H'_i / H'_{i - 1}$, then $K_i \cong K'_{\sigma(i)}$)

For example, the following two series are equivalent:
$$\left\{ e \right\} \overset{\mathbb{Z}}{\trianglelefteq} \: 15 \cdot \mathbb{Z} \: \overset{\mathbb{Z}_5}{\trianglelefteq} \: 3 \cdot \mathbb{Z} \: \overset{\mathbb{Z}_3}{\trianglelefteq} \mathbb{Z}$$
$$\left\{ e \right\} \overset{\mathbb{Z}}{\trianglelefteq} \: 15 \cdot \mathbb{Z} \: \overset{\mathbb{Z}_3}{\trianglelefteq} \: 5 \cdot \mathbb{Z} \: \overset{\mathbb{Z}_5}{\trianglelefteq} \mathbb{Z}$$

Theorem (6.10) (The Jordan-Holder theorem) (משפט ג'ורדן-הולדר):
Every two composition series of a group $G$ are equivalent.
Proof:
Let
$$\left\{ e \right\} = H_0 \trianglelefteq H_1 \trianglelefteq \dots \trianglelefteq H_r = G$$
$$\left\{ e \right\} = K_0 \trianglelefteq K_1 \trianglelefteq \dots \trianglelefteq K_s = G$$
Be composition series of $G$.
By [[#^2c3f3a | The Schreier refinement theorem]] we have that they have an equivalent refinements. By definition, the quotients $H_i / H_{i - 1}$ and similarly for $K_i / K_{i - 1}$. And thus there is no non-trivial refinement for them. Therefor, the quotients in the refined series, that are not trivial, are exactly the quotients from the original series. And since we have a permutation between those quotients, we conclude that the number of non-trivial quotients in both is the same, meaning $r = s$, and that the quotients from the original series are mapped by that permutation one to one between themselves. So we have a permutation between the original quotients, and that by definition means that the original series are equivalent.
(Note, in the refinement, a quotient is non trivial iff it is one of the quotients from the original series it refined) ^6d06a4

Corollary (6.12) (The fundamental theorem of arithmetic):
Every natural number is a product of prime numbers, and up to order, that product is unique.
Proof:
Let $n \in \mathbb{N}$. And consider the group $G = \mathbb{Z}_n$.
>1) We will show that $n$ is a product of primes.
	By [[סדרות הרכב#^fbb6d3 | Every finite group has a composition series]] we have a composition series for $G$. Because $G$ is abelian, every factor in that composition is abelian. And according to [[חבורות פשוטות#^475f7c | A simple finite abelian group is of prime order]] we get that every factor is of prime order. And by [[סדרות הרכב#^5d3900 | The order of a group is a product of the orders of the factors in a composition series]] we have that the order of $G$ (which is $n$) is a product of primes.

^5a7519

>2) We will show that the representation of $n$ as a product of primes is unique:
>	Assume that $n = p_1 \cdot p_2 \cdots p_r$ where $p_i$ is prime $\forall i \in [r]$ ($i \neq j \cancel\implies p_i \neq p_j$).
>	Then we get that
>	$$\left\{ e \right\} = <p_1 \cdot p_2 \cdots p_r> \overset{\mathbb{Z}_{p_1}}{\trianglelefteq} <p_2 \cdot p_3 \cdots p_r> \overset{\mathbb{Z}_{p_2}}{\trianglelefteq} $$
>	$$\overset{\mathbb{Z}_{p_2}}{\trianglelefteq} <p_3 \cdot p_4 \cdots p_r> \overset{\mathbb{Z}_{p_3}}{\trianglelefteq} \cdots \overset{\mathbb{Z}_{p_{r-2}}}{\trianglelefteq} <p_{r-1} \cdot p_r> \overset{\mathbb{Z}_{p_{r-1}}}{\trianglelefteq}$$
>	$$\overset{\mathbb{Z}_{p_{r-1}}}{\trianglelefteq} <p_r> \overset{\mathbb{Z}_{p_r}}{\trianglelefteq} <1> = G$$
>	Is a composition series of $G$.
>	And by [[#^6d06a4 | The Jordan-Holder theorem]] we know that those factors are the same as the factors in the series from [[#^5a7519 | Part 1 of this proof]]. Thus the same $p_i$-s are in both representations of $n$ as a product of primes. $\blacksquare$
